Meet the Team

Faculty leadership and graduate assistants advancing immersive storytelling for middle-school STEM learning.

Faculty

Leadership

Xiaoxia "Silvie" Huang, PhD

Associate Professor · Instructional Design, Development & Evaluation

Xiaoxia "Silvie" Huang is an associate professor in Instructional Design, Development, and Evaluation in School of Education at Syracuse University. Her research centers on designing, developing, and evaluating technology-supported environments in various formal and informal settings to facilitate cognitive, affective, and motivational processes in learning. Her most recent research efforts focus on designing and evaluating theory-based immersive virtual reality learning environments.

Brice Nordquist, PhD

Dean's Professor of Community Engagement · Writing & Rhetoric

Brice Nordquist is a community-engaged writing and rhetoric researcher and teacher. He works through participatory research and humanities- and arts-based programming to study and support students' movements across contexts of learning and stages of education. He is the co-founder and co-director of the Narratio Fellowship, a storytelling program for resettled refugee youth, and the founder and director of Syracuse University's Engaged Humanities Network.

Sharon Dotger, PhD

Professor of Science Education · Faculty Director, Teacher Education

Sharon Dotger is a Professor of Science Education and the Faculty Director of Teacher Education and Undergraduate Studies in School of Education at Syracuse University. She researches teaching methods that optimize science learning for all students and collaborates with teachers in lesson study.

Dan Pacheco, Professor

Peter A. Horvitz Chair of Journalism Innovation · Newhouse School

Dan Pacheco is the Peter A. Horvitz Chair of Journalism Innovation at the Newhouse School, and considered a pioneer in using virtual reality for journalism. With a 30-year background in digital publishing—from Washington Post to AOL and startups—he has led numerous innovations in online communities, social networking, and emerging media platforms.

Matthew Potteiger, Professor

Professor · Landscape Architecture, SUNY ESF

Matthew Potteiger is a Professor in the Department of Landscape Architecture at the SUNY College of Environmental Science and Forestry (ESF). His teaching, research, and community engagement center on the cultural practices that shape landscapes, with current work linking food and landscape systems through community-based projects aimed at building more sustainable and socially just food systems. This work includes co-founding Syracuse Grows, an urban agriculture advocacy group, developing a peri-urban farm with New American refugees, and creating Central New York's first regional food system plan, Food Plan CNY. In 2025, he received the American Society of Landscape Architects' highest honor, the Community Service Honor Award, recognizing his long-term commitment to engaging diverse communities locally and internationally. He is the co-author, with Jamie Purinton, of Landscape Narratives: Design Practices for Telling Stories .

Nick Bowman, PhD

Associate Professor · S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communication

Nick Bowman is an Associate Professor in the S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communication. His research explores user experiences in interactive technologies, such as metaverse technologies and video games. He has published more than 150 peer-reviewed articles and holds affiliate faculty positions in Canada, Mexico, and Taiwan.

Stewart Diemont, PhD

Associate Professor · Environmental Biology, SUNY ESF

Stewart Diemont is an Associate Professor in the Department of Environmental Biology at SUNY College of Environmental Science and Forestry. His research and teaching focus on environmental analysis, modeling, and ecosystem design. He has 20 years' experience working with Indigenous Maya forestry and agriculture, and with communities in Mesoamerica; 10 years in environmental evaluation and cultural system documentation in North America and Europe, emphasizing food systems in urban areas.
